# Community-Acquired Pneumonia (CAP): Rapid Evidence Review 2025–2026

**Authors:** IMBR Study Room  
**Date:** March 2026  
**Sources:** ATS 2025 Guidelines, IDSA/ATS 2019, UCSF IDMP, AAFP 2022

## Abstract

Community-acquired pneumonia (CAP) remains a leading cause of infectious disease mortality worldwide, with an annual incidence of 248 per 100,000 adults. This review synthesizes the latest 2025 ATS guidelines with established IDSA/ATS 2019 recommendations, providing updated evidence on diagnosis, empiric antibiotic therapy, duration of treatment, and prevention strategies.

---

## 1. Epidemiology

- **Annual incidence:** 248 cases per 100,000 adults
- **Incidence increases dramatically with age:**
  - Ages 65–79: 634 per 100,000
  - Ages ≥80: 16,430 per 100,000
- **30-day mortality:** 6% for hospitalized patients; 34% for those with treatment failure
- **Hospitalization rate:** 9× higher in COPD patients
- **Microbiology (hospitalized CAP):**
  - No pathogen identified: 62%
  - Virus only: 23%
  - Bacteria only: 11%
  - Mixed bacteria + virus: 13%

**Common bacterial pathogens:**
| Pathogen | Frequency |
|----------|-----------|
| *Streptococcus pneumoniae* | Most common |
| *Haemophilus influenzae* | Common |
| *Mycoplasma pneumoniae* | Common (atypical) |
| *Chlamydia pneumoniae* | Common (atypical) |
| *Legionella* spp. | 3% of hospitalized |
| *Moraxella catarrhalis* | Less common |
| Respiratory viruses | 23% |

## 2. Diagnosis

### Clinical Presentation
- **Classic symptoms:** Cough, fever, chills, sputum production, dyspnea, pleuritic chest pain
- **Signs:** Tachypnea, tachycardia, hypoxia, altered mental status (especially elderly)
- **High diagnostic odds ratios:**
  - Physician's overall clinical impression (OR = 11.5)
  - Egophony (OR = 6.5)
  - Any abnormal vital sign (OR = 6.0)
  - Tachypnea (OR = 3.1)
  - Measured fever (OR = 3.3)

> ⚠️ **Elderly patients may NOT present with fever**

### Imaging
- **Chest X-ray (CXR):** Standard for radiographic confirmation
  - PPV only 26.9% vs CT (NPV 96.5%)
- **CT chest:** More accurate but cost/availability limited
- **Lung Ultrasound (LUS):** Now acceptable alternative when CXR/CT not readily available (ATS 2025)
  - More accurate than CXR per meta-analysis
  - Requires trained operator

### Laboratory Testing
| Test | Recommendation |
|------|---------------|
| CBC | Often elevated WBC |
| Sputum culture | NOT routine; for severe CAP, MRSA/Pseudomonas risk factors |
| Urine antigen tests | NOT routine; for severe CAP |
| Procalcitonin | NOT recommended (IDSA) |
| Influenza/COVID testing | If flu-like symptoms during high-activity season |
| MRSA nares | If empiric vancomycin initiated (high negative predictive value) |

## 3. Severity Assessment

### CURB-65 Score

| Criterion | Points |
|-----------|--------|
| **C**onfusion (new onset) | 1 |
| **U**rea > 7 mmol/L | 1 |
| **R**espiratory rate ≥ 30/min | 1 |
| **B**P < 90/60 mmHg | 1 |
| Age ≥ **65** years | 1 |

| Score | Risk | Action |
|-------|------|--------|
| 0–1 | Low (mortality 0.5–5%) | Outpatient |
| 2 | Moderate (mortality 5.1%) | Consider hospitalization |
| 3–4 | High (mortality 18.9%) | Hospitalize; consider ICU |

### PSI/PORT Score
- Validated 20-variable score
- Use as adjunct to clinical judgment (IDSA recommendation)
- Available at: mdcalc.com/psi-port-score-pneumonia-severity-index-cap

## 4. Treatment

### Outpatient Empiric Therapy

#### Previously Healthy, No Antibiotics in Past 3 Months
| Option | Dose | Notes |
|--------|------|-------|
| **Doxycycline** ⭐ | 100 mg PO BID | **Preferred** |
| Amoxicillin | 1 g PO TID | Alternative |
| Macrolide* | Standard dose | Only if local macrolide resistance <25% |

*Macrolide options: Azithromycin, Clarithromycin

#### Comorbidities OR Antibiotic Use in Past 3 Months
**Comorbidities:** Chronic heart/lung/liver/renal disease, DM, alcoholism, malignancy, asplenia, immunosuppression

| Regimen | Dose | Notes |
|---------|------|-------|
| **Doxycycline + Amoxicillin** ⭐ | 100 mg BID + 1 g TID | **Preferred combination** |
| Doxycycline + Cefpodoxime | 100 mg BID + 200 mg BID | Alternative combination |
| **Levofloxacin** (monotherapy) | 750 mg PO daily | Alternative; contact ASP if requiring approval |

> ⚠️ **ATS 2025 Update:** For outpatients WITH comorbidities who test positive for respiratory virus (influenza/COVID): consider NOT adding empiric antibiotics if clinically stable — to reduce unnecessary antibiotic use.

### Inpatient Empiric Therapy

#### Non-Severe CAP
- **Beta-lactam + Macrolide** OR
- **Beta-lactam + Doxycycline**

**Beta-lactam options:** Ceftriaxone, Cefotaxime, Ampicillin-sulbactam  
**Macrolide:** Azithromycin (500 mg daily × 3 doses for atypicals)

#### Severe CAP
- **Combination therapy required:**
  - Beta-lactam + Macrolide OR
  - Beta-lactam + Doxycycline

**If MRSA risk factors (prior isolation, recent hospitalization + parenteral antibiotics):**
- Add: Vancomycin OR Linezolid

**If Pseudomonas risk factors:**
- Add: Piperacillin-tazobactam, Cefepime, Ceftazidime, Imipenem, Meropenem, OR Aztreonam

> 💡 **Rule:** Obtain cultures BEFORE escalating coverage to allow de-escalation if pathogen not confirmed

#### Special Populations

**Influenza with CAP:**
- Add Oseltamivir 75 mg PO BID × 5 days
- Reduce dose in renal insufficiency
- ICU/immunocompromised/obese patients may need higher doses/prolonged therapy

## 5. Duration of Antibiotic Therapy

### ATS 2025 Key Change — Shortened Duration

| Setting | Recommended Duration |
|---------|---------------------|
| Outpatient (non-severe, stable) | **3–5 days** |
| Inpatient non-severe CAP (stable) | **3–5 days** |
| Confirmed MRSA | ≥7 days |
| Confirmed Pseudomonas | ≥7 days |
| Legionella suspected | 7 days |
| Typical uncomplicated | Minimum 5 days |

**Stop antibiotics when:**
- Afebrile 48–72 hours
- Stable (HR ≤100, RR ≤24, SBP ≥90, O₂ sat ≥90%, alert)

> ⚠️ CXR and cough abnormalities may persist **up to 6 weeks** — NOT a valid reason to extend antibiotics

## 6. Corticosteroids in CAP

> ⚠️ **Evidence has evolved significantly** — landmark trials now support specific steroid use in severe CAP with demonstrated mortality benefit for certain agents.

### 6.1 Landmark Trials

| Trial | Year | Agent | Population | Key Finding |
|-------|------|-------|------------|-------------|
| **CAPE COD** ⭐ | 2022 | Hydrocortisone 200 mg/day IV | Severe CAP (n=800) | **Mortality: 6.2% vs 11.9%** (p=0.006); absolute reduction −5.6%. Most robust evidence for hydrocortisone |
| **ESCAPe** | 2016 | Methylprednisolone 0.5 mg/kg/day | Severe CAP (n=585) | No mortality benefit; reduced need for mechanical ventilation |
| **Santeon-CAP** | 2022 | Dexamethasone 6 mg/day | CAP (n=401) | No mortality benefit; reduced ICU admissions in severe subgroup |
| **SONIA** | 2024 | Low-dose glucocorticoids | CAP in low-resource setting | 16% reduction in 30-day mortality |
| **Lancet Respir Med Meta-analysis** (8 RCTs) | 2024 | Various | Hospitalized CAP | **28% reduction in 30-day mortality** (OR 0.72) |
| **BMJ Open Resp Research Meta-analysis** | 2024 | Various | Severe CAP (multiple RCTs) | **30% reduction in all-cause mortality** (RR 0.70) |

### 6.2 Which Steroid? Evidence by Agent

| Agent | Mortality Benefit in Severe CAP? | Evidence Quality |
|-------|--------------------------------|-----------------|
| **Hydrocortisone** ⭐ | **YES — most consistent** | CAPE COD RCT + multiple meta-analyses |
| Methylprednisolone | No mortality benefit; reduces MV need | ESCAPe negative for mortality |
| Dexamethasone | No mortality benefit shown | Santeon-CAP negative; NMA inconclusive |

> 💡 **Hydrocortisone is the agent with the most consistent mortality benefit** in severe CAP

### 6.3 Selection Criteria — Who Should Receive Steroids?

**Indications (ATS 2025 — conditional; SCCM 2024 — strongly recommend):**

✅ **Severe CAP defined as:**
- Requiring invasive mechanical ventilation, OR
- Septic shock requiring vasopressors, OR
- High-flow nasal cannula with FiO₂ ≥50% and PaO₂/FiO₂ <300, OR
- PSI Class V (score >130), OR
- ≥3 minor criteria (RR ≥30, PaO₂/FiO₂ <250, multilobar infiltrates, confusion, BUN ≥20, WBC <4,000, platelets <100K, hypothermia)

✅ **High inflammatory state:**
- **CRP ≥ 200 mg/L at admission** — strong predictor of mortality benefit from steroids

✅ **Early administration:**
- Within 24 hours of meeting severe CAP criteria

**Expected Benefits in Selected Patients:**
- ↓ 28-day/30-day mortality (hydrocortisone: −5.6% absolute)
- ↓ Need for mechanical ventilation
- ↓ Hospital length of stay
- ↑ Faster shock reversal (if septic shock present)

### 6.4 Contraindications & Cautions

| Situation | Recommendation |
|-----------|----------------|
| **Non-severe CAP** | ❌ NOT recommended (ATS 2025 strongly against) |
| **Influenza pneumonia** | ❌ Avoid — increased mortality, impaired viral clearance |
| **Fungal pneumonia** | ❌ Avoid — no benefit in observational data |
| **High-dose short-duration** (>400 mg hydrocortisone equiv. <3 days) | ❌ Not recommended for septic shock — increased adverse effects without benefit |
| **Uncontrolled hyperglycemia** | ⚠️ Monitor closely; steroids worsen glucose control |
| **GI bleeding risk** | ⚠️ Stress ulcer prophylaxis recommended |

### 6.5 Dosing Regimen (Based on CAPE COD + SCCM 2024)

| Component | Recommendation |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Agent** | **IV Hydrocortisone** preferred |
| **Dose** | 200 mg/day — either 50 mg IV q6h OR continuous infusion |
| **Duration** | 5–7 days (CAPE COD used 4–7 days then taper) |
| **Tapering** | Consider tapering if on >7 days to avoid adrenal insufficiency |
| **Alternative** | Methylprednisolone 40–80 mg/day IV if hydrocortisone unavailable |

### 6.6 Recommendations by Setting

| Setting | Recommendation | Strength |
|---------|---------------|---------|
| Non-severe CAP (inpatient) | **Against** — do not use | Strong (ATS 2025) |
| Severe CAP — no shock | **Suggested** if CRP ≥200 or meets severe criteria | Conditional (ATS 2025) |
| Severe CAP with septic shock | **Suggested** | Conditional (ATS 2025) |
| Refractory septic shock | Consider adding | Weak recommendation |
| Influenza pneumonia | **Avoid** | Strong against |

> **SCCM 2024 Focused Update:** Strongly recommends corticosteroids for severe bacterial CAP hospitalization

## 7. Prevention

### Pneumococcal Vaccination (2022 Updated Guidelines)

| Population | Vaccine Recommendation |
|-----------|----------------------|
| Adults ≥65 years | **PCV20 alone** OR **PCV15 + PPSV23** (1 year later) |
| Ages 19–64 with comorbidities | **PCV20 alone** OR **PCV15 + PPSV23** (1 year later) |
| Previously received PCV13 only | No additional PCV20/PCV15 needed |
| Previously received PPSV23 only | Give PCV15 or PCV20 **1 year after PPSV23** |

> PCV13 no longer routinely recommended for immunocompetent adults ≥65 years

**PPSV23 efficacy:** 13% relative risk reduction in CAP; 28% reduction in adults ≥65 or high-risk younger adults

### Other Vaccinations
- **Influenza:** Annual vaccination recommended for all adults
- **COVID-19:** Per current CDC guidelines

## 10. Severe CAP — ICU Management

### 10.1 Definition & ICU Admission Criteria

Severe CAP requires ICU admission when **≥1 major criterion** OR **≥3 minor criteria** are met (IDSA/ATS 2019, endorsed ATS 2025).

**Major Criteria (require ICU):**
| Criterion | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Septic shock | Hypotension requiring vasopressors |
| Respiratory failure | Requiring invasive mechanical ventilation |

**Minor Criteria:**
| Criterion | Threshold |
|-----------|-----------|
| Respiratory rate | ≥ 30 breaths/min |
| PaO₂/FiO₂ ratio | ≤ 250 |
| Multilobar infiltrates | ≥ 2 lobes involved |
| Confusion/disorientation | New onset with this illness |
| Uremia | BUN ≥ 20 mg/dL |
| Leukopenia | WBC < 4,000 cells/mm³ |
| Thrombocytopenia | Platelets < 100,000 cells/mm³ |
| Hypothermia | Core temperature < 36°C |
| Hypotension | Requiring aggressive fluid resuscitation |

> ⚠️ **10–30%** of hospitalized CAP patients require ICU treatment

---

### 10.2 Empiric Antibiotic Therapy in Severe CAP

**First-line combination therapy (mandatory):**

| Component | Options | Dose |
|-----------|---------|------|
| Beta-lactam | Ceftriaxone, Cefotaxime, or Ampicillin-sulbactam | Standard doses |
| PLUS Macrolide | Azithromycin or | 500 mg daily |
| OR | Doxycycline | 100 mg BID |

**MRSA Coverage** — add if: prior MRSA isolation, recent hospitalization + parenteral antibiotics, or local MRSA risk factors:
- Vancomycin OR Linezolid

**Pseudomonas Coverage** — add if: prior Pseudomonas isolation, recent hospitalization + parenteral antibiotics, or structural lung disease:
- Piperacillin-tazobactam, Cefepime, Ceftazidime, Imipenem, Meropenem, OR Aztreonam

> 💡 **Critical:** Obtain blood + sputum cultures BEFORE starting broad-spectrum antibiotics — allows targeted de-escalation when results return

---

### 10.3 Respiratory Support

**Severe Hypoxia (PaO₂ < 55 mmHg despite O₂ or hypercapnia with pH < 7.35):**
→ Intubation + Mechanical ventilation

**Before intubation — consider:**
| Modality | Indications | Notes |
|---------|-------------|-------|
| **HFNC** | Mild-moderate hypoxemic respiratory failure | Better oxygenation than standard O₂; reduces intubation need |
| **NPPV** | Hypercapnic or severe hypoxemic respiratory failure | May reduce intubation rate; use with caution in sepsis |

**Lung-protective ventilation (if ARDS develops):**
- Low tidal volumes: **6 mL/kg ideal body weight**
- Target plateau pressure < 30 cm H₂O
- Prone positioning for PaO₂/FiO₂ < 150
- Consider neuromuscular blockade for 24–48h if PaO₂/FiO₂ < 150

---

### 10.4 Hemodynamic Support

**Septic Shock from Severe CAP:**

1. **Fluid resuscitation:** 30 mL/kg crystalloid bolus, then reassess
2. **Vasopressors:** Start if hypotension persists after fluid
   - **Norepinephrine** — first-line
   - Vasopressin — add if needed
   - Target MAP ≥ 65 mmHg

3. **Corticosteroids (ATS 2025 — conditional):**
   - Consider if severe CAP with high inflammatory markers
   - Consider if ICU admission, complicated respiratory failure
   - Give within 24 hours of meeting severe CAP criteria
   - Evidence: may reduce ARDS risk, no definitive mortality benefit

### 10.5 Complications & Outcomes

**Common Complications:**
| Complication | Frequency/Notes |
|--------------|----------------|
| ARDS | Major cause of mortality; occurs in ~30% of severe CAP |
| Empyema | Pleural effusion with infection |
| Lung abscess | Necrotizing pneumonia |
| Septic shock | Requires vasopressors |
| Multisystem organ failure | Kidney, liver, coagulation dysfunction |
| Cardiac complications | MI, arrhythmia, heart failure |

**Mortality:**
| Setting | Mortality Rate |
|---------|---------------|
| ICU patients | **21–55%** |
| In-hospital (severe CAP) | Up to **56%** |
| 1-year post-discharge | Up to **40.7%** |

> ⚠️ Long-term survival is significantly worse than matched controls — driven by recurrent CAP episodes and cardiovascular events

### 10.6 Risk Factors for Severe CAP & Mortality

| Risk Factor | Impact |
|-------------|--------|
| Age ≥ 60–65 years | Independent mortality risk |
| COPD | 9× higher hospitalization; increased ICU need |
| Chronic heart/liver/renal disease | Increased mortality |
| Diabetes mellitus | Increased complications |
| Alcoholism | Increased severe CAP risk |
| Obesity (BMI > 30) | Independent risk factor |
| Prior respiratory infection (<1 year) | Increased recurrence risk |
| Delay in antibiotics | **Every hour delay ↑ mortality** |
| Bacteremia | Higher complication rate |

**Pathogens associated with severe CAP:**
- *Streptococcus pneumoniae* (most common, including DRSP)
- *Staphylococcus aureus* (including MRSA)
- *Legionella pneumophila*
- *Pseudomonas aeruginosa*
- Gram-negative enteric bacilli
- Polymicrobial infections → worse prognosis

### 10.7 Key Differences: Severe vs. Non-Severe CAP

| Aspect | Non-Severe CAP | Severe CAP (ICU) |
|--------|---------------|-----------------|
| **Setting** | Outpatient / Ward | ICU |
| **Antibiotics** | Doxycycline ± Amoxicillin | Beta-lactam + Macrolide/Doxycycline |
| **MRSA/Pseudomonas** | Not routine | Add if risk factors |
| **Corticosteroids** | Against (ATS 2025) | Suggested if criteria met (ATS 2025) |
| **Cultures** | Not routine | Blood + sputum mandatory |
| **Urine antigens** | Not routine | *Legionella* + *S. pneumoniae* antigens |
| **Duration** | 3–5 days if stable | 5–7 days minimum; longer if complicated |
| **Mortality** | < 10% | 21–55% (ICU) |

## 11. Take-Home Points

> 1. **Diagnose CAP** with clinical features + radiographic infiltrate (CXR, CT, or LUS)
> 2. **Assess severity** with CURB-65 to guide outpatient vs. inpatient decision
> 3. **Start empiric antibiotics** promptly: Doxycycline for most outpatients; combination therapy for comorbidities
> 4. **Shorten duration** to 3–5 days if clinical stability achieved (ATS 2025)
> 5. **De-escalate** when cultures return; stop broad-spectrum coverage if MRSA/Pseudomonas not confirmed
> 6. **No corticosteroids** for non-severe CAP; consider for severe CAP (ATS 2025 conditional)
> 7. **Prevent** with PCV20/PCV15 + PPSV23 vaccination in appropriate populations
> 8. **Severe CAP = ICU** when ≥1 major criterion or ≥3 minor criteria (IDSA/ATS)
> 9. **Severe CAP: Beta-lactam + Macrolide** is mandatory first-line; add MRSA/Pseudomonas coverage only with risk factors
> 10. **Time is life** — every hour delay in antibiotics increases mortality in severe CAP
