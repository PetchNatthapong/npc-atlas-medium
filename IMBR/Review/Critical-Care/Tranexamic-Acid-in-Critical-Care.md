# Tranexamic Acid in Critical Care

## Abstract

Tranexamic acid (TXA) is an antifibrinolytic agent that has revolutionized the management of hemorrhagic conditions in critical care. Originally developed in the 1960s, TXA gained widespread attention following the CRASH-2 trial in 2010. This review article provides a comprehensive appraisal of landmark trials, current guidelines, and clinical applications of TXA in critical care settings, including trauma, surgery, obstetrics, and specific populations.

---

## 1. Mechanism of Action

### Pharmacodynamics

Tranexamic acid is a synthetic derivative of the amino acid lysine. Its antifibrinolytic effect works by:

| Mechanism | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Competitive inhibition** | Binds reversibly to lysine binding sites on plasminogen |
| **Prevents plasminogen activation** | Blocks conversion of plasminogen → plasmin |
| **Stabilizes fibrin clot** | Prevents fibrinolysis and clot breakdown |

### Key Differences from Aprotinin

| Feature | Tranexamic Acid | Aprotinin |
|---------|-----------------|-----------|
| Mechanism | Direct plasminogen inhibition | Serine protease inhibition |
| Risk of thrombosis | Lower | Higher |
| Cost | Low | High |
| Evidence base | Large (CRASH-2) | Limited |

## 2. Landmark Trials — Comprehensive Appraisal

### 2.1 CRASH-2 Trial (2010) — Trauma Hemorrhage

**Large, Randomized, Placecho-Controlled Trial in Trauma**

| Parameter | Result |
|-----------|--------|
| **N** | 20,211 adult trauma patients |
| **Intervention** | TXA 1g IV over 10 min, then 1g over 8 hrs vs placebo |
| **Primary Outcome** | All-cause mortality at 28 days |
| **Result** | **Reduced mortality**: 14.5% vs 16.0% (RR 0.91, 95% CI 0.85-0.97) |
| **Number Needed to Treat (NNT)** | 67 patients to prevent 1 death |
| **Key Finding** | Greatest benefit when given within **3 hours** of injury |

**CRASH-2 Mortality Benefit by Time:**

```
< 1 hour post-injury:  32% reduction in death
1-3 hours:              21% reduction in death
> 3 hours:             No benefit (may increase mortality)
```

**Appraisal:**
- ✅ Large sample size (n=20,211)
- ✅ Robust methodology (RCT, placebo-controlled, multicenter)
- ✅ Clinically meaningful outcome (mortality)
- ✅ Clear time-dependent benefit
- ⚠️ Industry-independent funding (but wide confidence intervals)

---

### 2.2 CRASH-3 Trial (2019) — Traumatic Brain Injury

| Parameter | Result |
|-----------|--------|
| **N** | 12,737 patients with mild-moderate TBI |
| **Result** | **Non-statistically significant** reduction in head injury-related death |
| **Subgroup Analysis** | Possible benefit in severe TBI with GCS ≤8 |
| **Safety** | No increase in adverse events |

**Appraisal:**
- ✅ Large sample size
- ⚠️ Primary outcome not met (statistically non-significant)
- ⚠️ Heterogeneous population (mild to moderate TBI mixed)
- ✅ Post-hoc analysis suggests benefit in severe TBI
- ⚠️ Effect size smaller than CRASH-2

---

### 2.3 WOMAN Trial (2017) — Postpartum Hemorrhage

| Parameter | Result |
|-----------|--------|
| **N** | 20,060 women with PPH |
| **Result** | Reduced death from PPH (1.5% vs 1.9%, p=0.03) |
| **NNT** | ≥ 500 women (less effective than in trauma) |
| **Key Finding** | Greatest benefit when given **within 3 hours** of childbirth |

**Appraisal:**
- ✅ Large sample size
- ✅ Statistically significant result
- ⚠️ NNT much higher than trauma (500 vs 67)
- ✅ Clear benefit in high-risk obstetric population
- ⚠️ Effect size modest

---

### 2.4 PATCH-Trauma Trial (2023) — Prehospital TXA

| Parameter | Result |
|-----------|--------|
| **N** | 1,310 severely injured patients (Australia, NZ, Germany) |
| **Setting** | Pre-hospital administration in advanced trauma systems |
| **Primary Outcome** | Survival and functional recovery at 6 months |
| **Result** | 4 additional survivors per 100 patients at 6 months |
| **Safety** | No increased vascular occlusive events |

**Critical Finding:**
> "Survivors were found to be severely disabled and highly dependent on caregivers"

**Appraisal:**
- ✅ First large pre-hospital RCT
- ⚠️ Quality of life outcome concerning (survivors more disabled)
- ✅ Confirmed mortality benefit at 24 hrs and 28 days
- ⚠️ Raises questions about functional outcomes, not just survival
- ⚠️ Only 4 countries (limited generalizability)

---

### 2.5 HALT-IT Trial (2024) — GI Bleeding

| Parameter | Result |
|-----------|--------|
| **N** | 12,009 patients with acute GI bleeding |
| **Result** | **NO evidence** of mortality benefit |
| **Safety Signal** | **Increased risk of seizures and VTE** |
| **Recommendation** | TXA should NOT be used for GI bleeding outside RCT |

**Appraisal:**
- ✅ Large, well-designed RCT
- ✅ Clearly negative result (no benefit)
- ⚠️ Safety concerns (seizures, VTE)
- ⚠️ Contradicts earlier smaller meta-analyses
- ✅ Practice-changing (ACG guidelines now recommend against)

---

### 2.6 POISE-3 Trial (2022) — Non-Cardiac Surgery

| Parameter | Result |
|-----------|--------|
| **N** | 9,535 adults undergoing non-cardiac surgery |
| **Result** | TXA reduced major bleeding by ~25% (9% vs 12%) |
| **Safety** | No increase in composite cardiovascular events |
| **Subgroup** | Consistent effects across surgery types |

**Appraisal:**
- ✅ Large surgical RCT
- ✅ Significant bleeding reduction
- ✅ No cardiovascular safety concerns
- ✅ Generalizable to broad surgical population

## 3. Systematic Reviews & Meta-Analyses

### 3.1 Surgery Meta-Analyses

| Year | Studies | N | Finding |
|------|---------|---|---------|
| 2012 | 129 trials | 10,488 | ↓ Blood transfusion by **38%** |
| 2013 | Multiple | Various | ↓ Blood loss by **~30%**, 1g dose sufficient |
| 2022 | 47 meta-analyses | Umbrella | ↓ Transfusion, ↓ re-operation, no ↑ death/VTE |
| 2025 | Low-risk surgery | Various | Significant reduction in blood loss and transfusion |

### 3.2 Key Takeaways from Meta-Analyses

1. **Consistent benefit** across surgery types (orthopedic, cardiac, abdominal, spinal)
2. **Dose-response**: 1g total dose appears sufficient for most adults
3. **No increased mortality** or vascular occlusive events (reassuring)
4. **Seizure risk** identified in cardiac surgery (noted but not prohibitive)

## 4. Current Guidelines (2024-2026)

### 4.1 NICE Guidelines — Updated 2025-2026

**National Institute for Health and Care Excellence**

| Setting | Recommendation |
|---------|----------------|
| **Surgery with bleeding risk** | Offer TXA if breach of skin/mucosa in operating theatre |
| **Outside operating theatre** | Consider TXA if expected blood loss >500 mL |
| **Trauma** | Continue current practice per CRASH-2 protocol |
| **Pediatric surgery** | Draft guidance extending to children (2025) |

### 4.2 EAST Guidelines — Updated 2025

**Eastern Association for the Surgery of Trauma**

| Setting | Recommendation | Evidence |
|---------|---------------|----------|
| **Pre-hospital TXA** | Conditional recommendation | Low-quality evidence |
| **In-hospital TXA** | Conditional recommendation | Significant mortality reduction at 24 hrs and 1 month |
| **Dosing** | 1g bolus + 1g infusion over 8 hrs (standard) | CRASH-2 protocol |

### 4.3 NAEMSP/ACEP/ACS-COT Position Statement (2025)

**Prehospital TXA Administration**

| Recommendation | Details |
|----------------|---------|
| **Adult trauma with hemorrhagic shock** | Recommend prehospital TXA |
| **Timing** | Administer within **3 hours** of injury |
| **Pediatric trauma** | Role not extensively studied; if used, within 3 hours |
| **Safety** | Low risk of thromboembolic events and seizures |

### 4.4 ACG Guidelines — GI Bleeding (2023-2024)

| GI Bleeding Type | Recommendation |
|-------------------|---------------|
| **Upper GI bleeding** | Possible mortality reduction (mixed evidence) |
| **Lower GI bleeding** | **Strong recommendation AGAINST** antifibrinolytics |

## 5. Standard Dosing Protocol

### 5.1 Adult Dosing (Based on CRASH-2)

```
╔════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╗
║  TRANEXAMIC ACID — STANDARD ADULT PROTOCOL                 ║
╠════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╣
║  Loading dose:   1 gram IV over 10 minutes                 ║
║  Maintenance:    1 gram IV over 8 hours                    ║
║  Total dose:    2 grams over 8 hours                      ║
║                                                            ║
║  Alternative (hemorrhagic shock):                          ║
║  • 1g IV bolus over 10 min, repeat once over 8 hrs        ║
║  • Maximum: 2 grams total                                 ║
╚════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╝
```

### 5.2 Pediatric Dosing

| Weight | Loading Dose | Maintenance |
|--------|--------------|-------------|
| <50 kg | 20 mg/kg IV over 10 min | 10 mg/kg/hr over 8 hrs |
| ≥50 kg | Adult dosing | Adult dosing |

### 5.3 Special Populations

| Population | Consideration |
|------------|---------------|
| **Renal impairment** | Reduce dose (TXA is renally excreted) |
| **History of seizures** | Relative contraindication (TXA lowers seizure threshold) |
| **DIC** | Relative contraindication |
| **Pregnancy** | Can use in PPH (WOMAN trial data) |

## 6. Contraindications & Cautions

### Absolute Contraindications

| Contraindication | Reason |
|------------------|--------|
| Hypersensitivity to TXA | Risk of anaphylaxis |
| Active venous or arterial thrombosis | Risk of extension |
| Subarachnoid hemorrhage | Theoretical risk of vasospasm |

### Relative Contraindications

| Caution | Clinical Concern |
|---------|------------------|
| **History of convulsions** | TXA can precipitate seizures |
| **Severe renal impairment** | Drug accumulation |
| **DIC** | May worsen coagulopathy |
| **History of thromboembolism** | Risk of recurrence |

### Adverse Effects

| System | Adverse Effect | Frequency |
|--------|----------------|-----------|
| **GI** | Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea | Common |
| **CNS** | Seizures (especially with high dose/rapid infusion) | Rare |
| **CV** | Hypotension (with rapid IV push) | Rare |
| **Thromboembolic** | DVT, PE (low risk per CRASH-2) | Rare |

## 7. Clinical Applications in Critical Care

### 7.1 Trauma Hemorrhage

**Algorithm for TXA in Trauma:**

```
Trauma Patient with Bleeding
           │
           ▼
    Hemorrhagic shock?
    (SBP <90, HR >110)
           │
     ┌─────┴─────┐
     │           │
     ▼           ▼
    YES         NO
     │           │
     ▼           ▼
Give TXA      Monitor closely
within 3 hrs  Consider TXA if
              significant bleeding
              develops
```

### 7.2 Surgical Blood Conservation

| Surgery Type | Evidence | Recommendation |
|--------------|----------|----------------|
| **Cardiac surgery** | Mixed results | Consider in high-risk patients |
| **Orthopedic surgery** | Effective for reducing blood loss | Recommend |
| **Spinal surgery** | Effective | Recommend |
| **Major abdominal surgery** | Limited data | Case-by-case |

### 7.3 Obstetric Hemorrhage

- **Indication:** Postpartum hemorrhage (PPH) >500 mL or ongoing bleeding
- **Timing:** Within **3 hours** of childbirth
- **Dosing:** Same as adult protocol (1g loading + 1g maintenance)
- **Evidence:** WOMAN trial showed reduced mortality

### 7.4 Massive Transfusion Protocol

TXA is recommended as part of massive transfusion in:

| Protocol | Role |
|----------|------|
| **1:1:1 (PRBC:FFP:Platelets)** | TXA added as 1g loading dose |
| **Critical bleeding protocols** | Early administration within 3 hours |

### Comparison: TXA vs Other Antifibrinolytics

| Feature | Tranexamic Acid | Aminocaproic Acid |
|---------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Potency** | Higher | Lower |
| **Dosing frequency** | Lower (longer half-life) | Higher |
| **Evidence** | Extensive (CRASH-2, etc.) | Limited |
| **Cost** | Low | Low |
| **Clinical use** | First-line | Alternative |
